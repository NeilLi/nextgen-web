Trust and Governance

Governance belongs inside assistant action, not bolted on after it.

SeedCore is designed for environments where assistants publish and purchase on behalf of owners only after delegated authority, policy, provenance, custody, and risk checks pass.

Deny by DefaultDelegated AuthorityReplayable Receipts
Trust Model Every action should be explainable, reviewable, recoverable, and attributable.

That principle shapes how SeedCore handles owner policy, assistant scope, custody state, merchant trust, and post-action replay.

Control Model

Core trusted-agency controls.

Owner and Assistant SeparationThe assistant never becomes the owner.

Owner twins hold policy authority, while assistant twins hold only delegated scope and runtime identity.

Policy-Gated Publish and BuyExecution is gated through explicit trust, provenance, budget, and custody contracts.

Policy is enforced as machine-checkable runtime logic, not advisory prompt text.

Receipts and ReplayEvery action records policy rationale, execution outcome, and linked evidence.

This supports internal audit, merchant review, partner trust, and dispute investigation.

Deployment Posture

How SeedCore approaches assistant trust.

  • Parallel deploymentSeedCore can sit beside existing assistant, commerce, and publishing systems rather than forcing platform replacement.
  • Minimal blast radiusScoped delegation, short-lived execution tokens, and revocable permissions reduce uncontrolled assistant behavior.
  • Counterparty-ready trustMerchants, partners, and owners can verify what happened from the same replayable receipt trail.
