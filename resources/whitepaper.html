Authority Doctrine

The paper formalizes three distinct sources of authority.

The whitepaper makes the separation explicit so enterprise teams can reason about safety, accountability, and execution without ambiguity.

AICognition and judgment

AI remains the advisory layer for interpreting events, synthesizing telemetry, and proposing plans. It does not receive direct execution authority.

AgentIdentity and accountability

The Governed Agent is the accountable principal. It holds identity, privilege, and execution responsibility across the runtime.

RobotEmbodiment and actuation

The robot or endpoint is the constrained physical actuator. It executes signed, time-bounded, policy-approved actions only.

Five Surfaces

Five governed surfaces separate AI judgment from physical movement.

Together, these surfaces define the runtime architecture described across the latest SeedCore documentation.

Event Ingress

Normalize telemetry, provenance scans, operator requests, and environment signals into a governed event stream before planning begins.

Policy Layer

Apply identity, zoning, timing, release, and custody rules before any action is released to a Governed Agent or endpoint.

Execution Routing

Dispatch to specialized Governed Agents based on privilege, capability, and risk rather than letting a generic model act directly.

Playback and Audit

Provide black-box forensics for every custody transition, policy decision, and endpoint action across the full mission path.

Exception Recovery

Trigger quarantine, rollback, fallback, and escalation loops automatically when seals break, handlers block, or policy conflicts emerge.

Rare Assets

Rare asset scenarios are a proving ground for the architecture.

A significant portion of the whitepaper is dedicated to high-stakes workflows such as rare wild tonics, where provenance, release control, and custody evidence directly affect trust and inventory value.

Why This MattersRare-asset workflows punish ambiguity.

The runtime must verify origin, authorize movement, supervise handlers, and preserve chain-of-custody evidence to prevent million-dollar inventory loss, partner disputes, and audit failures.
