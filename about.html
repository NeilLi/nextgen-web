Infrastructure Thesis

SeedCore is building the trust infrastructure for AI assistants acting on behalf of owners.

The category shift is deliberate: assistants are moving from answering to doing, which means publishing, purchasing, and release decisions now need delegated authority, policy gating, custody-aware state, and replayable accountability.

Trusted AgencyDelegated AuthorityReplayable Receipts
Positioning Shift From creator tooling and workflow software to the trust substrate for owner agents.

SeedCore sits between owner intent and assistant execution, governing authority, publish and buy policy, custody, and replay.

Mission

Make assistant-driven action governable, accountable, and commercially usable.

SeedCore exists to close the gap between owner intent and assistant action through delegated authority, zero-trust policy, custody-aware digital twins, and challenge-ready replay.

AuthorityAssistants should never act with ambiguous permission.

We do not rely on broad account access or prompt discipline to represent owner authority.

StateEvery important actor and object should have a governed twin.

Owners, assistants, productions, products, sellers, and shipments all need policy-active state.

ReplayEvery sensitive action should be challenge-ready.

Receipts and custody history make publishing and purchasing defensible when disputes or trust checks arise.

Operating Principles

Trusted agency, not autonomy theater.

Owner intent is primary

The owner defines scope, budgets, risk tolerances, and approval rules. The assistant acts within that boundary.

Assistants are governed actors

An assistant is not the owner. It is a delegated principal with explicit scope, identity, and review history.

Policy gates all sensitive action

Publishing, release, purchase, and transfer should be denied by default until trust conditions pass.

Replay is part of the product

Receipts, evidence lineage, and policy rationale are first-class outputs, not afterthoughts.
